Day 2

Reading Charts — Candlesticks & Trends

The Candlestick

Each candle tells you the story of that time period:

     |        ← "wick" — the HIGH reached during the period
  ┌──┴──┐
  │     │  ← the "body" — from open price to close price
  └──┬──┘
     |        ← "wick" — the LOW reached during the period

Green candle = price went UP (close > open)  |  Red candle = price went DOWN (close < open)

Key Candle Patterns (Week 1 Only)

  • Long wick on top of red body — Price shot up, then was REJECTED. Sellers in control. Don't buy here.
  • Long wick on bottom of green body — Price dropped hard then BOUNCED. Buyers stepped in. Potential entry.
  • Doji (tiny body, long wicks) — Nobody in control. Market is undecided. Wait.
  • Big green candle, no wick — Strong buying pressure. Momentum is up.

Timeframes

TimeframeWhat it's good for
1 minuteNoise. Ignore for now.
15 minutesShort-term entries
1 hourMedium-term direction (your bot uses this)
4 hoursTrend confirmation
1 DayBig picture — where is price overall?
Top-Down Analysis Rule: Always check 1D first (big picture) → 4H (medium trend) → 1H (entry timing). Never start on the 1-minute chart.

Trend Direction

Uptrend: Price making higher highs AND higher lows. Look to buy dips.
Downtrend: Price making lower highs AND lower lows. Stay out or wait.
Sideways range: Bouncing between two levels. Buy near the bottom, sell near the top.

Never trade against the trend on your first week. If it's going up, only look to buy dips. If it's going down, stay out entirely.
Pull up BTC/USDT on Bybit. Switch to 1D chart. Ask: "Uptrend, downtrend, or sideways?" Then 4H. Then 1H. Is the answer consistent? Repeat for SOL/USDT and ETH/USDT.

Day 3

Technical Indicators — The Bot's Language

Your AI bot uses all of these. Now you'll understand what it's doing.

RSI — Relative Strength Index (Scale: 0–100)

Measures whether a coin has been bought or sold too much recently.

100 ─────────────────── Overbought zone (above 70)
 70 - - - - - - - - - -
 50 ··················· Neutral
 30 - - - - - - - - - -
  0 ─────────────────── Oversold zone (below 30)
  • RSI > 70 = Coin is overbought. Likely to pull back. Risky to buy.
  • RSI < 30 = Coin is oversold. May bounce. Potential buy opportunity.
  • Note: In a strong uptrend, RSI can stay above 70 for a LONG time. Use with other signals.

MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence

Tells you: is the momentum speeding up or slowing down? Has two lines (MACD + Signal) and a histogram (bars).

  • MACD crosses ABOVE Signal line → Potential buy signal
  • MACD crosses BELOW Signal line → Potential sell signal
  • Histogram growing bigger → Momentum accelerating in that direction

Moving Averages (EMA)

  • EMA 20 — Last 20 candles average. Reacts fast.
  • EMA 50 — Last 50 candles average. Medium speed.
  • EMA 200 — Big picture trend.

Golden rule: Price above EMA 200 = generally bullish. Price below = caution.

EMA 20 crosses above EMA 50 = "Golden Cross" → bullish  |  Crosses below = "Death Cross" → bearish

Bollinger Bands

Three lines: middle (20-period MA), upper band, lower band.

  • Price touches upper band = Overbought zone. May pull back.
  • Price touches lower band = Oversold zone. May bounce.
  • Band squeeze (bands very close) = Low volatility. A BIG move is coming. Wait for direction, then follow.

Support & Resistance

Support = A price floor where buying repeatedly stopped price from falling further.
Resistance = A price ceiling where selling repeatedly stopped price from rising further.

Golden rule: When support breaks, it becomes resistance. When resistance breaks, it becomes support. This is one of the most reliable patterns in all of trading.
Open BTC/USDT 1H chart. Add these free indicators: RSI (14), MACD (12,26,9), EMA 20, EMA 50. Spend 30 minutes reading them. Where is RSI? Is MACD above or below signal? Is price above EMA 50?
Day 4 — Most Important

Risk Management — The Day That Will Save Your Life

The 2% Rule

Never risk more than 2% of your account on a single trade.

With $50: max risk = $1 per trade.

This feels tiny. It IS tiny. That's the point. You need to be able to lose 50 trades in a row before you're broke. That gives you 50 chances to learn. Nobody loses 50 trades in a row if they apply any logic at all.

How to Calculate Your Position Size

You need to know three things before entering any trade:

  1. Entry price — where you're buying
  2. Stop loss price — where you'll admit you're wrong and exit
  3. Max loss — 2% of account = $1
Position Size = Max Loss ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Loss Price)

Example:
  BTC at $82,000 | Stop loss at $81,000 | Max loss = $1
  Position = $1 ÷ ($82,000 − $81,000) = $1 ÷ $1,000 = 0.000001 BTC

Yes, that's tiny with $50. That's correct. This is how you learn without blowing up.

Risk/Reward Ratio — Only Take Trades Worth Taking

For every $1 you risk, you should have potential to make $2. Minimum 1:2 ratio.

You only need to be right 40% of the time if your risk/reward is 1:2.
You can lose MORE than you win and still be profitable. Let that sink in.

Day 5

Entry & Exit Strategy

Entry — When to Pull the Trigger

Anticipation entry: "Price is at support, I think it will bounce." Higher reward, higher risk.
Confirmation entry: "Price bounced AND next candle closed green AND RSI turned up." Wait for evidence.
For week 1: always use confirmation entries.

The 6-Point Setup Checklist

Look for all of these. When 4 out of 6 are true, it's a reasonable trade:

  1. Price is in an uptrend (higher highs, higher lows on 4H)
  2. Price pulled back to a support level
  3. RSI is between 40–60 (not overbought)
  4. A green confirmation candle closes at support
  5. Volume is decent (not unusually low)
  6. MACD is above signal line or crossing up

Stop Loss Placement

Place it below the support level you're buying at. If price breaks through, your thesis is wrong — exit.

Place stop a little below support, not right at it. Price often dips slightly through support ("stop hunt") before bouncing. Give it room.

Take Profit

At the next resistance level. If no clear resistance exists, use 2× your stop distance as minimum.

Trailing Stops (What Your Bot Uses)

You buy BTC at $82,000. Price rises to $84,000 (+2.4%). A 1% trailing stop means your stop is always 1% below current price.

  • Price $84,000 → stop at $83,160
  • Price rises to $85,000 → stop moves up to $84,150
  • Price reverses to $84,150 → SOLD. Profit locked in automatically.

Your bot uses trailing_stop_positive_offset: 0.02 — trailing stop activates after +2% profit.

Exit Rules (When to Get Out)

  • Stop loss hits — Non-negotiable. Never move your stop down. Ever.
  • Take profit hits — Take the money.
  • Your reason for entering is gone — e.g., uptrend broke. Exit even if stop hasn't hit.
Do NOT exit because you're scared or bored. Have a reason.
